Chapter 1: The Smith Family

John Smith was a respected merchant in the town. His wife Mary Smith managed the household.
Their son William Smith had just returned from university. Their daughter Elizabeth Smith
was engaged to be married.

Elizabeth's fiance was Thomas Brown, son of the wealthy banker Richard Brown.
Richard Brown's wife Catherine Brown was known for her charity work.

William Smith often visited his friend James Wilson. James was the son of Doctor
Henry Wilson, who had treated John Smith for many years.

At the town gathering, Mary Smith spoke with Catherine Brown about Elizabeth's
upcoming wedding. John Smith discussed business with Richard Brown while
William Smith and James Wilson planned a hunting trip.

Thomas Brown presented Elizabeth Smith with a family heirloom ring that had
belonged to his grandmother. The Browns and Smiths would soon be united by marriage.
