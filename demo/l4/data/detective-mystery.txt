=== CASE FILE: THE ASHFORD MANOR MURDER ===

INVESTIGATING OFFICER: Detective Inspector Sarah Blackwood
CASE NUMBER: 2024-AMM-0847
DATE: October 15, 2024

================================================================================
VICTIM PROFILE
================================================================================

NAME: Lord Edward Ashford III
AGE: 67
OCCUPATION: Retired industrialist, philanthropist
RESIDENCE: Ashford Manor, Devonshire
TIME OF DEATH: Between 10:00 PM and 11:00 PM, October 14, 2024
CAUSE OF DEATH: Poisoning (belladonna extract) combined with blunt force trauma to the head
LOCATION: The study, ground floor of Ashford Manor

BACKGROUND:
Lord Ashford was a wealthy and influential figure in British society. He made his fortune
in textiles and manufacturing, retiring 15 years ago to manage his estates and charitable
foundations. Known for his sharp business acumen but also for making enemies along the way.
His health had been declining in recent years, leading to frequent arguments about the future
of his estate.

RECENT EVENTS:
- Three weeks ago: Lord Ashford announced plans to change his will
- Two weeks ago: Family meeting called to discuss estate matters
- One week ago: Heated argument with business partner overheard by staff
- Day of death: Dinner party with 6 guests at Ashford Manor

WITNESS STATEMENTS

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 1: James Harrison - Butler, employed 25 years]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I have served the Ashford family for a quarter century, and I can say with certainty
that the tension in this house has never been higher than in these past few weeks.

The evening began as any formal dinner would. Guests arrived at 7:00 PM sharp. I
personally greeted each one at the door. The weather that evening was dreadful -
heavy rain and strong winds from the north. I had to have extra towels ready for
the guests' coats.

The dinner itself was served at 7:30 PM in the main dining room. Mrs. Patterson,
our cook, had prepared Lord Ashford's favorite - roast pheasant with seasonal
vegetables. I noticed that Colonel Pemberton barely touched his food, which was
unusual for him. He seemed preoccupied, constantly checking his pocket watch.

At approximately 9:15 PM, I served after-dinner drinks in the drawing room. Lord
Ashford took his usual brandy. I prepared it myself from the decanter in the
drawing room - the same one that has been used for years. The decanter was full
when I poured, and I noticed nothing unusual about its contents.

Around 9:45 PM, I heard raised voices coming from the study. I recognized Lord
Ashford's voice and what sounded like Colonel Pemberton. I could not make out the
words, but the tone was angry. This was not the first time I had heard them argue
recently.

At 10:30 PM, I was polishing the silver in the pantry when Miss Eleanor came
rushing in, quite distressed. She said she had found her father slumped over his
desk in the study. I immediately went to investigate and found Lord Ashford as she
described - deceased.

I must note that Lord Ashford had been receiving threatening letters over the past
month. He showed me one of them last week. It was typed and unsigned, warning him
to "leave the past buried." He seemed more amused than concerned at the time.

TIME UNACCOUNTED FOR: 10:00 PM - 10:30 PM (was alone in pantry)

ADDITIONAL OBSERVATIONS:
- The garden door near the study was unlocked when I checked it that night
- Lord Ashford's safe was open when the body was discovered
- I saw Colonel Pemberton near the garden door around 10:15 PM

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 2: Tom Fletcher - Head Gardener, employed 12 years]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I wasn't at the fancy dinner, mind you. I was working late in the greenhouse,
preparing the orchids for the autumn show. Lord Ashford was particular about his
orchids - wanted them perfect for the county fair next month.

The greenhouse is about 40 meters from the main house, but I have a clear view of
the garden path and the side of the manor from there. The rain had let up by about
9:30 PM, so I stepped outside for a smoke.

That's when I saw something strange. A figure in dark clothing - looked like a long
coat - walking quickly along the garden path toward the study's French doors. This
was around 10:20 PM or thereabouts. I couldn't see the face clearly, but the walk
was distinctive - a slight limp on the left side. Colonel Pemberton has that same
limp from his war injury.

After I finished my cigarette, maybe 10 minutes later, I saw the same figure
hurrying back the way they came. They seemed agitated, walking faster despite the
limp. They disappeared around the corner toward the front of the house.

I thought nothing of it at the time - guests often take walks in the garden. But
now, knowing what happened... it's got me worried.

About the garden itself - I should mention that several of my medicinal herb plants
have gone missing over the past month. Including the belladonna. I reported it to
Lord Ashford two weeks ago, and he said he'd look into it. Never did find out what
happened to those plants.

TIMELINE:
- 9:30 PM: Stepped outside greenhouse for cigarette
- 10:20 PM: Saw dark figure heading toward study
- 10:30-10:35 PM: Saw same figure returning from study direction
- 10:45 PM: Heard commotion from main house, stayed in greenhouse

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 3: Dr. Marcus Whitfield - Family physician and dinner guest]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I have been Lord Ashford's personal physician for the past eight years. I was
invited to the dinner as both friend and medical advisor - Edward had been having
some health concerns lately, primarily heart-related.

The dinner was pleasant enough, though I noticed tensions between several of the
guests. Eleanor seemed distant, barely speaking to her cousin William. Victoria
Ashford - Lord Ashford's sister - made several pointed comments about the family
inheritance that seemed to upset everyone.

Lord Ashford himself appeared in good spirits during dinner. He ate well, drank
moderately, and was engaged in conversation. There was no indication of any
immediate health crisis. His color was good, pulse steady when I last checked
earlier that day.

After dinner, around 9:00 PM, I was in the library examining Lord Ashford's
collection of medical curiosities - he had some fascinating antique surgical
instruments. I was alone there until approximately 9:45 PM, when William Ashford
came in looking for a particular book.

At about 10:15 PM, I stepped out onto the terrace for some fresh air. The rain had
stopped, and I wanted to clear my head. I could see lights in the study from where
I stood, and I thought I heard voices, but I couldn't identify who was speaking.

When Eleanor discovered her father at 10:30 PM, I was one of the first to respond.
As a physician, I examined the body immediately. The symptoms suggested poisoning -
dilated pupils, flushed skin, rapid pulse that would have preceded death. The blow
to the head appeared to have been delivered post-mortem or very close to death.

My professional opinion: Lord Ashford was poisoned first, then struck on the head
either as he fell or immediately after. The poison was likely administered in the
previous hour, given the progression of symptoms. Belladonna fits the profile
perfectly.

TIMELINE:
- 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM: At dinner

- 9:45 PM - 10:00 PM: Conversation with William Ashford in library
- 10:00 PM - 10:15 PM: Still in library, reading
- 10:15 PM - 10:30 PM: On terrace
- 10:30 PM: Responded to Eleanor's discovery

[WITNESS 4: Eleanor Ashford - Victim's daughter, age 34]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

My father and I had a complicated relationship. He could be stubborn, demanding,
impossible at times. But he was still my father, and I loved him.

The dinner was Father's idea - he wanted to gather the family and some close
friends. He said he had important announcements to make. I assumed it was about
the will changes he had been hinting at for weeks.

During dinner, I sat across from Colonel Pemberton. He kept staring at Father in
a way that made me uncomfortable. There was something different about him tonight -
a coldness I hadn't seen before. When I asked if he was alright, he just smiled
and said everything would be "sorted out soon."

After dinner, I spent time with Aunt Victoria in the drawing room. She was telling
me about her trip to Florence last spring - going on and on about the art museums.
I confess I wasn't really listening. I was worried about what Father was planning
to announce.

Around 10:00 PM, I excused myself to use the powder room. On my way back, I passed
the study and heard voices - Father's and someone else's. I heard Father say
something like "I know what you did" and "I have proof." The other voice was too
muffled to identify, but it sounded like a man.

I didn't want to eavesdrop, so I returned to the drawing room. About 25 minutes
later, I decided to check on Father. That's when I found him... slumped over his
desk. His eyes were open but unseeing. The brandy glass was on the floor, broken.
There was a brass candlestick next to his head, covered in...

I screamed and ran to get help. The butler came first, then Dr. Whitfield. I
couldn't look anymore.

TIMELINE:
- 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM: At dinner
- 9:00 PM - 10:00 PM: In drawing room with Victoria Ashford
- 10:00 PM - 10:05 PM: Powder room
- 10:05 PM: Overheard conversation in study
- 10:05 PM - 10:30 PM: Returned to drawing room
- 10:30 PM: Discovered body

IMPORTANT: Eleanor stands to inherit 60% of the estate under the old will, but
would have received only 25% under the proposed new will.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 5: Colonel Arthur Pemberton - Retired military, family friend]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I've known Edward Ashford for over forty years. We served together in the military
briefly, then became business partners in the textile trade. Our relationship has
had its ups and downs, as any long partnership does.

I won't pretend that things were smooth between us recently. Edward had discovered
some... irregularities in our joint business dealings from years ago. He was
threatening to go public with information that could damage my reputation. We had
words about it, yes. But murder? I'm a decorated officer. I have my honor.

During dinner, I was seated between Mrs. Helena Ashford - William's wife - and
Victoria Ashford. The conversation was pleasant enough. I may have been distracted
- I was thinking about how to resolve matters with Edward without public scandal.

After dinner, I went to the billiard room with William Ashford. We played a few
games. I am not a young man anymore, and my leg was bothering me - the old shrapnel
wound from Suez acts up in damp weather. I stepped out around 9:45 PM for some air.

I will admit I went to the study to speak with Edward. I arrived around 10:05 PM.
He was alive and well when I arrived. We had a frank discussion about our
differences. I offered to pay back the money in question. He seemed to be
considering it. I left around 10:20 PM. He was sitting at his desk, very much
alive, when I walked out.

I did NOT kill Edward Ashford. Yes, we had disagreements. Yes, I was in the study.
But I left him alive. Someone else must have entered after me.

TIMELINE (SELF-REPORTED):

- 10:05 PM - 10:20 PM: In study with Lord Ashford
- 10:20 PM - 10:30 PM: Returned to billiard room

NOTE: Gardener's sighting at 10:20 PM of limping figure matches Pemberton's

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 6: William Ashford - Victim's nephew, age 42]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Uncle Edward and I were never particularly close. He always favored Eleanor, his
own daughter. But blood is blood, and family matters.

I came to dinner because Uncle Edward insisted. He said he had important news
about the family's future. Mother - that's Victoria Ashford - was quite anxious
about what he might say. She's always worried about being left out of the will.

The dinner was tense. Everyone pretending everything was normal when we all knew
something was about to change. Uncle Edward enjoyed that, I think - keeping us all
in suspense.

After dinner, I played billiards with Colonel Pemberton for a while. He's a decent
player, for a man his age, though his mind wasn't really on the game. He kept
looking at his watch and seemed restless.

Around 9:45 PM, the Colonel excused himself. Said his leg was bothering him and he
needed to walk it off. I stayed in the billiard room, practicing shots by myself.
Helena - my wife - came in around 10:00 PM and we talked for a bit about the
renovations we're planning for our London flat.

I didn't leave the billiard room until we heard Eleanor screaming. That must have
been around 10:30 PM. I rushed to the study with everyone else and saw... Uncle
Edward, dead at his desk.

I had no love lost for Uncle Edward, but I didn't kill him. I stood to gain nothing
from his death - under either the old or new will, my share was minimal.

TIMELINE:

- 9:45 PM - 10:00 PM: Alone in billiard room
- 10:00 PM - 10:30 PM: In billiard room with wife Helena

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[WITNESS 7: Victoria Ashford - Victim's sister, age 62]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

My brother Edward was a difficult man. Brilliant, yes, but difficult. We had our
share of disagreements over the years, particularly about Father's estate and how
Edward handled the family businesses.

But murder? How could anyone think I would harm my own brother? We argued, like
all siblings do. But I loved him, in my own way.

The dinner was delightful, as usual. Edward always did know how to host a party.
I was seated next to that charming Dr. Whitfield. We discussed Italian Renaissance
art - I had just returned from Florence, you know. The Uffizi was magnificent.

After dinner, I spent most of my time in the drawing room with Eleanor. Poor dear
seemed so anxious all evening. I tried to distract her with stories from my
travels. She excused herself around 10:00 PM, and I remained in the drawing room,
reading a novel from Edward's library.

I heard voices at some point - men arguing, it sounded like. But the drawing room
is on the other side of the house from the study. I couldn't make out who it was
or what they were saying.

When Eleanor screamed, I nearly fainted. My heart condition, you know. Dr.
Whitfield had to attend to me before he could even examine poor Edward.

THE 1998 INCIDENT:

Based on the threatening letter and Colonel Pemberton's statement, there appears
to have been a financial irregularity in 1998 involving joint Ashford-Pemberton
business dealings. Investigation of historical records suggests:

- In 1998, Ashford-Pemberton Textiles reported a significant loss
- Insurance claim was filed for "inventory damage"
- Later investigation was dropped for lack of evidence
- Lord Ashford retained documents suggesting the claim was fraudulent
- Colonel Pemberton was the primary beneficiary of the insurance payout

If Lord Ashford exposed this fraud, Colonel Pemberton would face:
- Criminal prosecution
- Disgrace and ruin
- Possible imprisonment

CASE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Lord Edward Ashford was murdered between 10:00 PM and 10:30 PM on October 14, 2024.

The murder method was two-fold:
1. Poisoning with belladonna extract (administered via brandy)
2. Blunt force trauma to the head (brass candlestick)

Key observations:
- The garden door was unlocked
- Muddy footprints with limping gait pattern lead to and from garden
- Colonel Pemberton admits to being in the study at 10:05-10:20 PM
- Gardener saw limping figure at 10:20 PM going TO study and 10:30-35 PM returning
- The poison was in the victim's personal brandy glass
- Belladonna plants were stolen from the garden 2 weeks prior
- Colonel Pemberton had both motive (exposure of fraud) and opportunity

The evidence strongly suggests the sequence of events:
1. Poison was placed in the brandy glass before or during the evening
2. Colonel Pemberton visited the study at 10:05 PM
3. An argument ensued ("I know what you did... I have proof")
4. Lord Ashford consumed poisoned brandy during the confrontation
5. As the poison took effect, he was struck with the candlestick
6. Colonel Pemberton fled via the garden door around 10:30 PM
7. Eleanor discovered the body at 10:30 PM

PRIME SUSPECT: Colonel Arthur Pemberton
- Motive: Prevent exposure of 1998 insurance fraud
- Opportunity: Admitted to being in study; gardener saw him leaving
- Means: Access to garden (knew about belladonna), strength to wield candlestick
- Physical evidence: Footprints match his gait pattern
